75 Bucket List Ideas
for Women

13 min read March 2026 Inspiration

A bucket list isn't a list of things society thinks you should do. It's a list of things you have been quietly dreaming about — the trips you've bookmarked, the skills you've told yourself you'd learn, the versions of yourself you haven't fully become yet.

These 75 ideas are a starting point. Some will resonate immediately. Others might surprise you. The best bucket list always contains at least one thing that makes you a little nervous.

Solo Travel
Go Somewhere — Just You

Solo travel for women has transformed over the last decade. The world is more accessible, communities are more connected, and the confidence that comes from navigating a foreign city alone is unlike anything else.

  • ✦
    Take a solo trip to a country where you don't speak the languageOrder coffee badly. Get lost. Find your way. That combination is exactly what growth feels like.
  • ✦
    Spend a weekend alone in a city you loveNo agenda. Museums if you want. Sleep in if you want. Eat dinner at the bar. Own every decision.
  • ✦
    Stay in a women-only hostel or retreat centerThe conversations that happen in these spaces are unlike anywhere else. Go once.
  • ✦
    Take yourself to a restaurant you've always wanted to tryA table for one is not sad. It is deliberate. Bring a book or don't. Enjoy every course.
  • ✦
    Road trip with no fixed itineraryPick a direction. Drive. Stop when something looks interesting. This is how you find things worth keeping.
  • ✦
    Travel somewhere purely because it's beautifulAmalfi Coast. Scottish Highlands. Kyoto in autumn. Somewhere that earns its reputation.
Adventure
Push Past Comfortable
  • ✦
    Complete a multi-day hikeThe Appalachian Trail, Cinque Terre, the W Trek in Patagonia. Your body is more capable than your doubts suggest.
  • ✦
    Learn to scuba diveOpen water certification takes a weekend. What's below the surface of the ocean will change how you see the world.
  • ✦
    Go skydivingTandem is perfect. The freefall lasts 60 seconds. The feeling stays with you for years.
  • ✦
    Try surfingYou will fall constantly at first. That's the point. Getting up every time is something worth knowing you can do.
  • ✦
    Climb something that scares youA mountain, a via ferrata, an indoor climbing wall that feels very tall. The view from the top hits differently when it cost you something.
  • ✦
    See the Northern LightsIceland, Norway, Finland. Standing under the aurora is one of the few things that actually lives up to every photo you've ever seen of it.
  • ✦
    Go on safariEast Africa — Kenya, Tanzania, Botswana. Watching wildlife at dawn with no fence between you is genuinely transformative.
Creativity
Make Something
  • ✦
    Write the book, essay, or story you've been carrying aroundIt doesn't need to be published. It needs to exist. Put it somewhere outside your head.
  • ✦
    Take a ceramics or pottery classThere is something deeply satisfying about making something with your hands. Beginner classes are everywhere.
  • ✦
    Learn to paint — seriouslyNot a glass of wine and a canvas. An actual course. Learn color theory, technique, composition. Make something you're proud of.
  • ✦
    Take professional photographs of something you loveA landscape, a city, a person. Learn enough about photography to capture what you actually see.
  • ✦
    Learn to danceSalsa, tango, swing, ballroom. Your body knows how. It's just waiting for permission.
  • ✦
    Start a garden from scratchVegetables, herbs, flowers — whatever calls to you. Watching something grow from seed is a particular kind of joy.

On building a list that's actually yours: The most common mistake women make with bucket lists is filling them with what looks impressive rather than what feels true. Your list should make you feel something — excitement, nerves, longing. If it just looks good on paper, it's not your list yet.

Wellness & Self
Come Back to Yourself
  • ✦
    Attend a week-long wellness or yoga retreatBali, Costa Rica, Portugal, Tuscany. Let someone else run the schedule. Be fully present somewhere beautiful.
  • ✦
    Try a silent meditation retreatThree days minimum. Sitting with yourself in silence is harder than any adventure — and more rewarding.
  • ✦
    Do a complete digital detoxOne week. No social media, no email, minimal phone. Discover who you are when no one is watching and nothing is performing.
  • ✦
    Learn to cook one cuisine really wellJapanese, French, Thai, Italian. Take a proper class. Understand why food is culture.
  • ✦
    Run a race — any distance5K, 10K, half marathon. Cross a finish line that required you to train for it. That feeling doesn't fade.
  • ✦
    Sleep under the starsReal camping, not a glamping resort. A sleeping bag, a clear sky, no light pollution. Let the scale of the universe recalibrate you.
Learning
Expand What You Know
  • ✦
    Learn a new language to conversational levelSpanish, Italian, French, Portuguese. Not perfect fluency — enough to have a real conversation. It changes how you travel.
  • ✦
    Take a course in something completely outside your fieldAstronomy, architecture, philosophy, economics. Curiosity is a skill. Keep it sharp.
  • ✦
    Learn a musical instrumentPiano, guitar, ukulele. It's humbling and exhilarating. Your brain will genuinely thank you.
  • ✦
    Read 50 books in a yearA book a week. Mix fiction and non-fiction. Include voices very different from your own. A year of serious reading changes how you think.
  • ✦
    Attend a world-class lecture or symposiumA TED conference, a university public lecture, an industry summit. Be in the room where ideas are being made.
Connection & Legacy
What You Leave Behind
  • ✦
    Plan a girls' trip — a real oneNot a bachelorette. A real trip with the women who know you best. A destination that requires passports.
  • ✦
    Mentor a younger woman seriouslyGive her what you wish someone had given you. That's legacy work that compounds.
  • ✦
    Record your family's storiesInterview your parents and grandparents on video. The stories in their memories are not written down anywhere. This is urgent.
  • ✦
    Write letters to the people who shaped youSend them. Being told you mattered is one of the most powerful things a person can receive.
  • ✦
    Volunteer for a cause that genuinely matters to youNot occasionally. Seriously. Give your skills, not just your money.
Experiences Worth Every Penny
Spend It on This
  • ✦
    Stay somewhere extraordinary — onceA Relais & Châteaux property, a Japanese ryokan, a cliff-side villa in Positano. Go all in. Once.
  • ✦
    Attend a world-class performanceThe Met Opera, the Royal Ballet, La Scala. Sit close enough to feel it.
  • ✦
    Take a hot air balloon ride at sunriseCappadocia, Turkey is the most spectacular location on earth for this. Book it.
  • ✦
    Eat at a Michelin-starred restaurantNot for the status. For the craft. Understanding what food can be at its highest level changes how you cook and eat forever.
  • ✦
    Commission a piece of artFind an artist whose work moves you. Ask them to make something for your home. Live with original art.
